AI Capability Map
Six domains. Four levels.

A reference guide to what AI fluency actually looks like at Vertical — described in plain language, not vague buzzwords. Use this to locate yourself, then switch to the Assessment tab.

01 Prompting — Communicating with AI effectively
Passive
Uses simple, single-sentence prompts. Accepts the first output without iteration.
User
Provides context, specifies format, and knows how to iterate when output misses the mark.
Fluent
Constructs structured, multi-part prompts using roles, examples, and constraints. Iterates systematically.
Builder
Develops reusable megaprompts and prompt templates. Designs prompt architectures for specific tasks or workflows.
02 Research & Synthesis — Gathering and making sense of information fast
Passive
Uses AI like a search engine — asks questions, reads answers.
User
Uses AI to summarise documents, compare sources, and extract key points from longer content.
Fluent
Designs research workflows. Triangulates across tools. Stress-tests outputs before using them.
Builder
Builds automated research systems — news trackers, digest pipelines, monitoring tools that run without continuous manual input.
03 Writing & Communication — Drafting, editing, adapting content
Passive
Asks AI to fix grammar or rephrase sentences.
User
Uses AI to draft, restructure, and adapt tone for different audiences and formats.
Fluent
Uses AI as a thinking partner for communication — structure, strategy, and voice, not just execution.
Builder
Creates reusable templates and style frameworks. Can define and reproduce a specific voice or communication approach at scale.
04 Workflow Design — Redesigning how you work
Passive
Uses AI reactively — when stuck or in a hurry.
User
Has identified 2–3 regular tasks where AI meaningfully saves time or improves output.
Fluent
Has actively redesigned parts of their work around AI. Asks "where does AI fit?" before starting, not after.
Builder
Designs workflow systems for others. Automates handoffs and eliminates categories of manual work.
05 Building — Creating tools and systems
Passive
Uses existing tools only, off-the-shelf, without customisation.
User
Customises tools for their context — saved prompts, custom instructions, adapted templates.
Fluent
Builds functional tools: megaprompts for specific tasks, prompt chains, structured workflows others can follow.
Builder
Creates automations, agents, or trackers that operate without continuous manual input.
06 Critical & Creative Thinking — Brainstorming, stress-testing, going beyond your own limits
Passive
Takes AI output at face value. Rarely questions accuracy or pushes for original ideas.
User
Uses AI to brainstorm options and spot-checks outputs. Aware that AI can be wrong or overly generic.
Fluent
Actively uses AI to stress-test ideas, find blind spots, and push thinking into territory they wouldn't reach alone.
Builder
Designs thinking frameworks and structured AI dialogues that consistently elevate output quality — for themselves and others.
